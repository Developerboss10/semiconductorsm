Concepts of Semiconductor

Semiconductors are materials whose conductivity is between that of conductors and insulators. They have unique properties that make them essential in modern electronics.

1. Types of Semiconductors

  • Intrinsic Semiconductors: Pure form of semiconductor without any significant dopant atoms present. Example: Silicon, Germanium.
  • Extrinsic Semiconductors: Doped semiconductors that alter their electrical properties.
    • N-Type: Doped with elements that have more electrons than the semiconductor. Example: Phosphorus doped Silicon.
    • P-Type: Doped with elements that have fewer electrons. Example: Boron doped Silicon.

2. Energy Bands in Semiconductors

Energy bands are ranges of energy that electrons may occupy in a solid. In semiconductors, the valence band is full while the conduction band is partially filled.

3. Band Gap

The band gap is the energy difference between the valence band and conduction band. It determines a semiconductor's electrical properties.

4. Charge Carriers

When energy is applied, electrons can jump from the valence band to the conduction band, creating holes (absent electrons) in the valence band.

5. PN Junction

A junction formed between P-type and N-type semiconductors, critical for diodes and transistors.

6. Diodes and Transistors

  • Diodes: Allow current to flow in one direction only.
  • Transistors: Used to amplify or switch electronic signals.
